My Favorite People



Menda Renuka Devi

Menda Renuka Devi , was born in 1986 in Andhra Pradesh. From a young age, she dreamed of becoming a teacher, a guiding light in the lives of others. However, she was married early in life and took on the responsibilities of family with courage and unwavering strength.Despite life's many challenges, she faced each one with grace and determination. She is the heart and soul of our family—our strength, our guide, and our greatest inspiration. In every role she has played—devoted wife, loving mother, true friend, and natural teacher—she has shown more love, patience, and selfless dedication.Though she has never received any formal awards, she has won the world for us with her sacrifices, her support, and her endless care. She has shaped our lives quietly but powerfully, managing everything with steady hands and a tender heart.Thank you, Maa, for being our pride, our blessing, and the light that leads us forward. Your love is the foundation of our home, and your spirit will always be our greatest strength.




Karnam Malleswari

Karnam Malleswari is a true trailblazer in Indian sports history—the first Indian woman to win an Olympic medal. Born on June 1, 1975, inVoosavanipeta, Andhra Pradesh, she began weightlifting at the age of 12 and quickly rose through the ranks with sheer determination and grit.Her crowning moment came at the 2000 Sydney Olympics, where she lifted a total of 240 kg (110 kg in snatch and 130 kg in clean and jerk) to win bronze in the 69 kg category. That single medal was India's only one at those Games, and it made her a national icon. Beyond the Olympics, her career is studded with achievements: World Champion in 1994 and 1995 in the 54 kg category. 29 international medals,including 11 golds. Arjuna Award (1994), Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna (1999), and Padma Shri (1999). After retiring, she continued to inspire by taking on leadership roles, including being appointed Vice-Chancellor of Delhi Sports University in 2021. She also founded the Karnam Malleswari Foundation to promote weightlifting and empower young athletes.



M.S Subbulakshmi

Madurai Shanmukhavadivu Subbulakshmi, born on September 16, 1916, in Madurai, Tamil Nadu, was a legendary Carnatic vocalist and one of India’s most revered musicians. Raised in a musically rich environment—her mother was a veena player—Subbulakshmi began performing at a young age and gave her first public concert at just 11.She trained under Semmangudi Srinivasa Iyer in Carnatic music and Pandit Narayanrao Vyas in Hindustani music, mastering both traditions. Her voice, known for its purity and emotional depth, captivated audiences across India and the world. Her accolades are staggering: - Bharat Ratna (1998) – India’s highest civilian honor. - Ramon Magsaysay Award (1974) – often called Asia’s Nobel Prize. - Padma Bhushan, Padma Vibhushan, and Sangeetha Kalanidhi, among many others. Subbulakshmi was not just a musician—she was a cultural ambassador, a philanthropist, and a symbol of humility. She passed away on December 11, 2004, but her voice continues to echo in temples, homes, and hearts.



Sudha Murty

Sudha Murty born on August 19, 1950, in Shiggaon, Karnataka, is an Indian author, educator, engineer, and philanthropist. She holds a B.E. in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from B.V.B. College of Engineering & Technology and a Master’s in Computer Science from the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore—both with gold medals.She broke barriers early on by becoming India’s first female engineer at TELCO (now Tata Motors), after boldly writing a letter to J.R.D. Tata about gender discrimination in hiring. Later, she co-founded the Infosys Foundation in 1996 and served as its Chairperson, leading numerous initiatives in education, healthcare, rural development, and public hygiene. Sudha Murty is also a prolific writer, known for her accessible and heartfelt storytelling in English and Kannada. Her books—like Wise and Otherwise, How I Taught My Grandmother to Read, Three Thousand Stitches, and Grandma’s Bag of Stories—have sold millions of copies and are beloved by readers of all ages.In 2024, she was nominated to the Rajya Sabha for her contributions to literature, education, and social work. She’s received numerous honors, including the Padma Shri (2006) and Padma Bhushan (2023). She’s married to N. R. Narayana Murthy, co-founder of Infosys.



Sai Pallavi Senthamarai Kannan

sai pallavi born on May 9, 1992, in Kotagiri, Tamil Nadu, is a celebrated Indian actress and dancer known for her work in Telugu, Tamil, and Malayalam cinema. She grew up in Coimbatore and completed her schooling at Avila Convent School. Despite earning an MBBS degree from Tbilisi State Medical University in Georgia, her passion for the arts led her to the film industry.Her breakout role came in 2015 as Malar in the Malayalam film Premam,which catapulted her to stardom.She followed it up with powerful performances in Kali, Fidaa, Maari 2, Love Story, Gargi, and Shyam Singha Roy. Known for portraying emotionally rich and strong female characters.she has won six Filmfare Awards. South and two SIIMA Awards.Sai Pallavi is also a gifted dancer, having participated in reality shows like Dhee Ultimate Dance Show. Despite not being formally trained, her expressive style and natural rhythm have made her a fan favorite.She’s admired for her authenticity, often appearing on screen without makeup and choosing roles that challenge societal norms.
